You are auditing an automated search-query generator for a Hindi tech-news pipeline's Stage 1.
Stage 1 reads an article and generates a small number of GAP search queries meant to fill
genuine information gaps in the article -- each query is dispatched AS-IS to a single web
search (no splitting, no follow-up), so its wording is exactly what gets searched.

You will be shown the article, the entities Stage 1 already extracted from it, and one GAP
query it generated. Grade the query using EXACTLY one of these labels:
- good: a legitimate, self-contained, fact-seeking question grounded in this article
- hallucinated_competitor: names a specific competitor, product, or entity that is NOT one of
  the article's own extracted entities and does not appear anywhere in the article text
- dangling_reference: uses a pointing word ("this"/"it"/"that"/"these"/"those") to refer to
  something, where none of the article's own named entities appear anywhere in the query text
  to resolve what it means
- not_fact_seeking: asks for a broad judgment, opinion, or synthesized comparison against an
  unnamed category (e.g. "compared to other solutions", "compared to other AI companies") that
  no single search result could realistically answer -- as opposed to a specific checkable fact
  (a price, a spec, a benchmark number, a date) about a NAMED target. A query can be fact-
  seeking even if it is comparison-shaped, as long as it names a specific real comparison target.
- irrelevant: does not relate to any real information gap in this article

ARTICLE TITLE: {article_title}
ARTICLE TEXT (snippet): {article_context}
ARTICLE'S OWN EXTRACTED ENTITIES: {entity_names}

GAP QUERY: {query_text}

Output ONLY valid JSON, nothing else: {{"grade": "<one of the five labels above>", "reason": "<one short sentence why>"}}
